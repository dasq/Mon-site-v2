Who is Farid?

Travelling has been my life since forever, and even before myself. My parents were born in Algeria and are both from the Berber family. One of the most famous group of the Berbers is named the Tuaregs who are known, among other things, for travelling and living in the desert of the Saharan. But they are just a small part of this big family...

Originally from the Maghreb (at least 10,000 BC), the Berbers expanded on the south to inhabit the whole Western North Africa and even more (Morocco, Algeria, Tunisia, Libya, Egypt, Mali, Niger, and Mauretania). Connections with the Scandinavians (Sami people) have even been found. The importance of the Berber culture in Arabic world is such that they are often miscalled Arabs. But beware if you do, they will have a pleasure reminding you the differences… =)

So this is almost unsurprisingly that my parents also moved from their birthplace: I was then born in the suburb of Paris. I grew up in a mixed environment between the French culture and the Arab-Berber culture: I was studying classical music in school and I was dancing (well, maybe not so much) on Oriental music at home.

After graduating in high school, I went to study engineering in Troyes and this was a key time of my life where I expanded my vision of Occidental culture. Maintaining my guitar classical studies, I opened myself to a new world playing the electric guitar in so many facets it was possible: rock, blues, funk, fusion, jazz, pop, experimental… Entering the big band of the university was a big part of it giving us the possibility to work every semester with a professional musician (Nicolas Genest, Daniel Casimir, Alexis Torres, Olivier Sens…) This was such a chance I had than sometimes I almost forget I was there graduating in IT engineering.

Actually, I was not always there in Troyes during my studies. I have been an exchange student in New Orleans, or an intern in Boston, or a “researcher” in London. Obviously, I always took the opportunity to join both my studies with my passion. I have been busking in the streets of London, studying the guitar with David Fiuczinsky in the rooms of Berklee and I even graduated in Music Management and Art Marketing in the University of New Orleans.

Finally, I completed my engineering school and followed the path the working life. Nevertheless, I kept myself involved in the music life in Paris and in France performing in several scenarios from Rap to Jazz. But I believe the last band I had in France was one of the most influent to me: Ethnomus. We played traditional music from middle-age occidental to Persian or even Indian folklore.

After 4 years working in Paris for world leading IT and financial auditing companies, I said stop! Those years were very strenuous for my peace of mind. I decided to take off the suit and to offer myself time to put things into perspective. I travelled only with a backpack and a guitar through Asia, North Africa, Oceania, and Occident. And I have been overwhelmed by the beauties of the nature and by the warm-heartedness of some people. This impacted my life forever!

During this travel, I have learned many things, probably even more things that the school or the jobs didn't teach me and which are fundamental. At my corporate times, I was starting to feel angry about everything in this world and believing that everything was getting wrong. But I can proudly say that there is Love everywhere around us! We need to keep ourselves opened to the others and I know this can be really difficult when submerged by the constraints of our modern world. Simple things can participate in our happiness. And caring about the others is one of the fundamental keys. Giving all my heart when playing became a crucial goal I always challenge myself to achieve.

My music is the result of the fundamental lessons drawn from my life which I share with jubilation. I hope this communicates my lifetime experience to show you the beauty of “going overseas”. It is not possible to always travel and the music has always my conveyance for the mind like pictures can be. As I am better expressing myself through the music, I invite you to listen to my compositions. If you can, please share with me your thoughts about the feelings would communicate you. Also, I will share with you the words of the farseeing philosophe Plato which they mean very much to me: “Music is a moral law. It gives soul to the universe, wings to the mind, flight to the imagination, and charm and gaiety to life and to everything.”
