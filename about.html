Who is Farid?

French and Algerian guitarist, I am most importantly a citizen of the world. The music I play is the result of the compassion I have to the many different and beautiful cultures we are surrounded with. Musically and spiritually, I got myself interested in various folklores playing the guitar, the bouzouki, the oud, the ukulele... Formerly trained as a classical and jazz guitarist, I now play a cosmopolitan modern fingerstyle guitar which mixes traditional music genres.

I started studying the classical guitar at the age of 6 years old and I completely felt in love with this instrument. The acoustic guitar is a form of musical expression which offers me an exciting and vast exploration of soulful music. Since around 3,000 years BC when the first form of the guitar has been revealed, this instrument has crossed the time and the cultures. So many great musicians have expressed themselves with this instrument giving their heart and sharing their various stories. And this will never stop!

I believe the world is full of love but it happened that I was lost in the craziness of the “modern life” I was living in. One day, I decided to take off the suit and to offer myself time to put things into perspective. I travelled only with a backpack and a guitar through Asia, North Africa, Oceania, and Occident. I have been overwhelmed by the beauties of the nature and by the warm-heartedness of some people. This impacted my life forever!

Love is everywhere around us and we need to keep ourselves opened to the others. Simple things can participate in our happiness. Caring about the others is one of the fundamental keys. And giving all my heart when playing is one crucial goal I always challenge myself to achieve.

My music is the result of the fundamental lessons drawn from my life and I share it with jubilation. I hope this communicates my lifetime experience to show you the beauty of “going overseas”. As I am better expressing myself through the music, I invite you to listen to my compositions. Although I will share with you the words of the mythic philosophe Plato: “Music is a moral law. It gives soul to the universe, wings to the mind, flight to the imagination, and charm and gaiety to life and to everything.”
